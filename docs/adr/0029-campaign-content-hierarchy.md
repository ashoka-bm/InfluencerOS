# Campaign Content Uses A Stable Ownership Hierarchy

InfluencerOS uses `Content Pillar -> Campaign -> Campaign Concept -> Concept Approval -> Project`. A Campaign retains one typed primary objective, one primary Pillar, one primary Audience Segment, and, for paid conversion, one primary offer; supporting Pillars, Segments, benefits, and free conversion assets are allowed. Campaign Concepts are stable testable hypotheses, Concept Approvals immutably authorize exact Project sets, and Projects remain one independently planned content unit with one owning Concept.

Campaigns use `draft`, `active`, `paused`, `completed`, and `archived`; Concepts use `draft`, `researching`, `ready_for_approval`, `active`, and `retired`. The initial Campaign Objective vocabulary is `awareness`, `audience_growth`, `trust_nurture`, `lead_generation`, `paid_conversion`, `customer_retention`, and `reactivation`. Changing a Campaign's primary objective or primary paid offer creates a new Campaign; materially changing a Concept's audience tension, promise, audience, or commercial hypothesis creates a linked Concept.

Campaign Revisions, Campaign Waves, assisted-Campaign attribution, and advanced outcome attribution are accepted future extensions but are not part of the initial implementation.
